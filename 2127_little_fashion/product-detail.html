Strong Passwords

Why you want a strong password

A good password will help keep your personal information secure, such as emails, files, or your financial information, from someone trying to get into your account.

How to make a strong password:

Make your passwords unique: reusing passwords is risky because if someone were to get your one password, they would be able to access your other accounts which may have sensitive information.

Make your password longer and more memorable: The longer a password is, the harder it is for someone to guess. We recommend at least 12 characters. You can use:

A lyric from a song or poem

A meaningful quote from a movie or speech

A passage from a book

A series of words that are meaningful to you

An abbreviation: Make a password from the first letter of each word in a sentence

Avoid passwords that can be easily guessed by people who know you or people looking at easily accessible information, like from your socials.

Avoid using personal information such as:

Your nickname or initials

The name of your child or pet

Important birthdays or years

The name of your street

Numbers from your address

Avoid common patterns and sequences such as:

Obvious words and phrases like "password"

Sequences like "abcd" or "1234"

Keyboard patterns like "qwerty" or "qazwsx"

Keep your passwords secure:

Use a password manager to store your passwords if you have trouble memorizing all of them or if they are all randomized

If you must write down passwords, keep them hidden somewhere secure
